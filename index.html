Scrum Master Involvement Across Stages

How the Scrum Master's role evolves as teams grow in maturity‚Äîfrom hands-on facilitation to autonomous agility.

Stage 1: Onboarding

Scrum Master is deeply involved‚Äîsetting up ceremonies, educating the team, and resolving blockers.

  • Facilitates stand-ups, planning, retrospectives
  • Introduces agile tools and rituals
  • Builds trust and psychological safety

Stage 2: Coaching

Scrum Master shifts to mentoring‚Äîencouraging ownership and guiding from the sidelines.

  • Team leads ceremonies with light guidance
  • Focus on agile maturity and velocity
  • Scrum Master coaches and supports

Stage 3: Autonomy

Team is self-organizing. Scrum Master steps back, focusing on strategic alignment.

  • Team runs all ceremonies independently
  • Scrum Master supports cross-team initiatives
  • Focus on scaling and continuous improvement

Scrum Master vs Team Involvement

Visualizing the shift in responsibilities across the development lifecycle.

Onboarding

Scrum Master is present daily, actively facilitating stand-ups, planning, retrospectives, and introducing agile tools. The focus is on building trust and psychological safety.

Coaching

Scrum Master reduces presence to 3 times a week, mentoring the team while encouraging ownership. Ceremonies are team-led with light guidance.

Autonomy

Scrum Master steps back to 1-2 times a week, supporting strategic alignment and cross-team initiatives. The team runs all ceremonies independently.

Agile Has Changed

Agile has adapted to remote work‚Äîembracing digital tools, async updates, and flexible workflows.

Remote Check-ins

Quick daily check-ins keep remote teams aligned, focused, and emotionally connected.

  • Held via Zoom, Teams, or Slack
  • Time-boxed and structured for clarity
  • Async options for global teams

Digital Kanban Boards

Cloud-based boards offer real-time updates, integrations, and seamless task tracking.

  • Visual task flow with drag-and-drop ease
  • Real-time updates for shared awareness
  • Integrated with Git, CI/CD, and team chat

Remote Collaboration

Teams collaborate across time zones using shared docs, async videos, and virtual retros.

  • Slack, Notion, Google Workspace
  • Shared docs and async video updates
  • Virtual retros and clear written comms

Before COVID

  • In-person stand-ups and retrospectives
  • Physical whiteboards and sticky notes
  • Office-based collaboration
  • Limited async communication

After COVID

  • Remote stand-ups via Zoom/Teams
  • Digital boards (Jira, Trello, Miro)
  • Hybrid and async collaboration
  • Global teams with flexible schedules
